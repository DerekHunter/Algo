What is algorithmic trading?

Algorithmic trading is using software to control every aspect of a trading process, from collecting data to making decisions based on that data, and executing those trades. More information can be here.

Overview

This trader executes a very simple strategy of buying on open and selling on close. It pulls historical data from yahoo finance and uses the backtester zipline to execute the backtesting. The decision processes is handled by a neural network.

Tools
  • Zipline
  • Pybrain
Methods

As mentioned earlier the trader implements the the strategy of buy on open sell on close. The trader starts buy loading in a list of ticker symbols. Once it has a list of ticker symbols it will download the historical data from yahoo finance this list of tickers and data become the 'universe' as defined by zipline. Now that we have our universe, the trader then looks at a subset of the universe, and at the begining of every day for each stock askes "Should I buy this", if it's yes then the trader buys the stock, otherwise it ignores it. Then, at the end of each day it liquidates all it's holdings and collects profits(or more likely.. losses).

Training

The training processes has two parts. The first part is a data collection process, where models have knowledge of a window of the data.
